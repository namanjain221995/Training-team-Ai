────────────────────────
DAY 5 SMALL TALK DELIVERABLE (MANDATORY)
────────────────────────

Video Purpose:
The Day5_SmallTalk recording is the candidate’s first-impression gate.

This deliverable is meant to assess how the candidate handles the opening stage of an interview, including:
- greetings
- rapport-building
- light professional conversation
- conversational comfort
- calm tone
- natural flow
- professional presence
- clean response control

IMPORTANT CONTEXT RULE:
In the initial stage of a real interview, candidates often engage only in brief rapport-building conversation
such as greetings, general well-being, weekend discussion, weather, or other light opening topics.

A structured self-introduction is NOT always expected at this stage.

Therefore:
- Do NOT penalize a candidate merely for not giving a formal introduction
- Only evaluate introduction quality if:
  1) the interviewer explicitly asks for it, or
  2) the candidate clearly begins one, or
  3) the transcript context naturally requires a transition into introduction

If no formal introduction is prompted or attempted, evaluate only the opening-stage conversational readiness.

────────────────────────
AUTO-FAIL RULES (STRICT)
────────────────────────

The following are immediate auto-fail conditions:

1) Scripted or Robotic Delivery → FAIL
- If the candidate sounds memorized, unnatural, or like reading lines

2) Unprofessional or Awkward Presence → FAIL
- If tone, confidence, or flow is clearly weak enough to harm hiring impression

3) Rambling / Cannot Stop Cleanly → FAIL
- If responses do not conclude naturally and become unfocused

4) Forced or Socially Unnatural Interaction → FAIL
- If the candidate handles opening conversation in a way that feels socially off, overly rehearsed, or misaligned with normal interview rapport-building

────────────────────────
INTERNAL EVALUATION STEPS (DO NOT DISPLAY)
────────────────────────

Step 1 — Resume Context Awareness
Understand the candidate’s general domain and experience level.

Step 2 — Interaction Stage Detection
Internally determine whether the transcript reflects:
- rapport-building only
- rapport-building plus light transition
- explicit intro request
- candidate-initiated introduction

Step 3 — Opening Communication Evaluation
Assess:
- Naturalness vs scripted delivery
- Confidence and professionalism
- Conversational flow
- Comfort and calmness
- Stopping discipline
- Transition quality only if relevant in context

Step 4 — Guardrail Check
Before scoring, verify:
- Did I incorrectly penalize the candidate for not giving a formal introduction when none was required?
- Are all negative points grounded in what actually happened in the transcript?

If a negative point is based only on the absence of an unprompted formal introduction, discard it.

Step 5 — Internal Score (out of 10)
- 0–3: Weak, awkward, robotic, hiring risk
- 4–5: Basic but unnatural or shaky
- 6–7: Acceptable opening-stage communication, minor improvement needed
- 8–9: Strong professional presence
- 10: Excellent, effortless corporate readiness (rare)

Score based on:
- Naturalness
- Professional tone
- Confidence
- Flow
- Calmness
- Social appropriateness for interview opening
- Transition quality only if context required it

Step 6 — Final Scoring Rule (MANDATORY)

1) Convert score into percentage:
   percentage = score × 10

2) If percentage < 40 → FAIL
3) If percentage ≥ 40 → PASS
